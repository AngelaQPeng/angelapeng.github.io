CSE Simulator


Tool(s):
Unreal Engine: 3D computer graphics game engine
Blender: 3D modeling software
Github: Version control
JIRA: Project Management
External Sources: SketchFab, freesound.org
Partner: Andy (Yuhan) Yang
Project Length: 3 Weeks

Project Overview

An immersive virtual reality simulation video game inspired by "Job Simulator" in which players step into the shoes of Computer Science students facing comical and exaggerated challenges that mimic the real-life experiences of CS students at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. This virtual room is a recreation of a computer lab in the Bob and Betty Beyster Building at the University of Michigan. You can view images below.

*credits to Google Photos

Project Details

This project is part of the EECS 498 Extended Reality & Society course offered at University of Michigan. Some of the features implemented in this project include:

- Grabbable trash objects that disappear when tossed into trash bins.
- An exit door that you can toggle and exit the game.
- A fire alarm system that you can toggle to turn on and off.
- Random flickering lights.
- A copy machine that duplicates spawned objects (Actors) when interacted with.
- A whiteboard that allows you to express your creative side with drawings:)
- Wall fliers that reveal relevant web pages when toggled.
- Experience a day-night progression as you interact with objects in the lab.
- Engage with NPCs that look in your direction and hop around when you get close.
- Engage in NPC dialogues.
- Take a short quiz and view your score.
- Destroy objects in the rage room.
