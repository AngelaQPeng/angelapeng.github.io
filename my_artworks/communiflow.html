CommuniFlow

üü° Project Overview

This application is designed to transform the way individuals engage with their community. With a user-centric approach, it offers an interactive experience, going beyond simple instant messaging. By integrating intuitive design, innovative features (i.e. find, save, and sign up for events, find interesting places and view corresponding posts, and find friends and invite/ accept invite to events from others), and a focus on fostering meaningful connections, it redefines community interactions.

Target Users: Individuals who value effective communication and meaningful connections within their local or online communities, fostering more interaction between friends and catering an enriching community experience.

Summary of Methods Used & Justification

The first method that we used in our study was the "Conceptual Landscape Method." In this approach, we instructed interviewees to visually illustrate their ideal user interface that would cater to their specific needs. After each interviewee created their basic diagram, we proceeded to ask about the rationale behind including each individual feature in their sketch. We choose this method due to the abundance of social media applications available in the market, each offering various features. Our aim was to gain insight into users' preferences and immediate thoughts when prompted to design a user interface that aligns with their desires.

The second method that we used was the ‚ÄúPersonal Inventory.‚Äù In this approach, we create detailed user personas by interviewing and collecting information about users' preferences, pain points, and motivations. We decided to use this method because it will help us understand which features or aspects of a product are most important to users and should be prioritized in design and development. Through asking our interviewees questions, we gathered personal inventory data to iterate on the future product, also improving the future product based on interviewees‚Äô feedback and reported experiences with other social media platforms.

The third method that we used was "A Day in the Life Method.‚Äù This approach involved assigning each team member the task of shadowing a selected interviewee for an entire day, with certain instances allowing for a partial day of observation. During these immersive experiences, we closely observed the interviewees' usage patterns, with a particular focus on their most frequently utilized social media platforms. The rationale behind adopting this method was to gain the most accurate and insightful understanding of user behavior and feature preferences. Through direct observation of our interviewee‚Äôs interaction with various features in various applications in the context of their daily lives, we aimed to uncover nuanced insights that may not be readily apparent through traditional surveys or interviews alone. This hands-on approach allowed us to bridge the gap between user feedback and real-world behavior.

Summary of Findings

1. Pain points/ Frustration
Through our meticulous observation and in-depth interviews, we have identified 7 user pain points within the current landscape of applications. The most frequently used applications, such as Instagram, WhatsApp, TikTok, Wechat, and RED, have been the focus of our research. Our findings reveal that while users appreciate certain features in each of these apps, none of them completely satisfy their needs. Consequently, users are inconvenienced by the necessity to switch between multiple social media platforms. One significant pain point we discovered is users' desire to segregate posts from celebrities or influencers from those of their friends. For instance, on Instagram, users follow a diverse range of people, ranging from friends and alumni to celebrities. However, all posts are displayed together on the home page, even when users may only want to view content from specific groups. Regrettably, there is currently no feature available to facilitate this level of customization or filtering.

Another prominent user pain point is the inadequacy of the recommendation systems in place. Users often struggle to find relevant content when searching for specific keywords or interests. This shortfall in content discovery hinders the overall user experience.

Many users feel a lack of real interaction between friends and communities. A recurring sentiment among interviewees was the perception of superficial connections through social media interactions. It often appears that likes, shares, and comments have taken precedence over fostering meaningful connections. It is apparent that there‚Äôs a desire for interactions that are genuinely authentic and go beyond the surface level. Furthermore, concerns regarding user privacy were raised. Data breaches have happened on many social media platforms in the past years as software development rapidly grows. As this technological growth happens, users are increasingly worried about misuse of their personal information.

2. Goal of Users
To address the user pain points mentioned earlier, we have identified 5 key objectives that we are committed to addressing and what we believe are the goals of our potential users. First, we aim to introduce a customizable content filtering feature, enabling users to easily categorize and access posts from specific groups. This will empower users to curate their feeds in alignment with their individual preferences.

Secondly, we hope to enhance the current state of art recommendation systems to provide users with more personalized content suggestions, ensuring that the content offered aligns better with their interests and preferences.
Third, we strive to improve user experience by giving them plenty of privacy options, to strengthen user privacy and security measures to protect user data and build trust. Provide robust privacy settings and educate users about them.
Fourth, we will prevent users from getting overwhelmed by the overloaded information by develop features that help users manage and filter the overwhelming amount of content on social media, such as customizable filters, topic prioritization, or "mute" options.

Fifth, we hope our users will find a sense of belonging in their community, by foster a sense of community through features like groups, forums, or events. Encourage meaningful interactions, discussions, and collaborations among users.

Furthermore, fostering authentic interactions among users. To achieve this, we plan to introduce features such as discussion forums, community groups, and other features that prioritize content from close friends and family in users' feeds. We are also exploring innovative solutions like location sharing and 'close friend bumps' to promote meaningful connections when friends are in proximity.

Last but not least, we are committed to implementing robust privacy controls and transparent data handling practices. We recognize the significance of protecting user data, and we will continue to prioritize data security and user privacy as integral aspects of our new social media application.

3. Context of Use
In our user research process for social media apps, we discovered that the context of use refers to the specific situations or scenarios in which users interact with the application. Understanding these contexts is vital for us to create a seamless and user-friendly experience. By thoroughly understanding these various contexts of use, we can create experiences that are more relevant, engaging, and enjoyable for our diverse user base. Here are the four points we learned from our research.

First, we have to consider how users engage with the app on different devices. Mobile users may have different needs and preferences compared to those using a desktop or laptop.

Second is the location when users are engaged in social media. Take into account where users are accessing the app. Most interviewees used social media in several locations, which can impact the type of content they seek and how they interact with others.

Third is the time of day, where timing also influences the user behavior. This can vary throughout the day. For instance, some users might be more active during their lunch break or in the evening. Therefore, we can tailor content and notifications accordingly.

Fourth is the privacy concerns that users often encounter when registering an account. Users have different comfort levels with sharing personal information. We need to respect their privacy preferences and provide clear options for controlling data sharing.

Functional & Usability Goals

1. Messaging: allows users to message individuals who are friends or someone they would like to reach out to.
2. Content Creation: allows users to create content such as video, image, text, links, music, etc.
3. Privacy and Security: ensure user data is protected and provide options for controlling who can see their content and interact with them.
4. Feedback and Error Handling: Provide clear feedback to users when they perform actions and offer helpful error messages when something goes wrong.
5. Notification: receive notification when the app isn‚Äôt open and allow widgets to be added to the home screen.
6. Wallet and payment: easy to track expenses, pay for bills, and receive payments seamlessly.

User Emotional Experience Goals

7. User Delight: Strive to delight users with unexpected and enjoyable experiences, such as personalized content recommendations, interactive features, or surprises on special occasions.
8. Reduce overload: allow users to filter content for viewing or select a specific recommended category of posts for viewing.
9. Community Engagement: Facilitate meaningful connections and friendships among users, promoting a sense of belonging.

Context of Use

10. Physical: Can be used anywhere at any time.
11. Social: Allow users to view friends, influencers, celebrities, and others‚Äô posts, content, and/ or feeds.
12. Organizational: Can be used for communication for work and personal use.
13. Technical: Can be used on any phone, tablet, or desktop application, as well as through computer web browsers.
